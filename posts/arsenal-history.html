Timeline of Arsenal's History

  • 1886: The Founding
    • Arsenal Football Club was founded by workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich, southeast London.
    • Originally named Dial Square, the club played its first match on December 11, 1886, against Eastern Wanderers and won 6–0.
    • The name was changed to Royal Arsenal shortly afterward.
  • 1893: Turning Professional
    • The club became professional and changed its name to Woolwich Arsenal.
    • Arsenal joined the Football League in 1893 as one of the first southern clubs in the league.
  • 1913: Relocation to Highbury
    • Facing financial difficulties, Woolwich Arsenal moved north across the city to Highbury, North London.
    • The club’s name was simplified to Arsenal.
  • 1930s: The Herbert Chapman Era
    • Under manager Herbert Chapman, Arsenal became one of England’s most successful clubs.
    • Key achievements:
      • Won the First Division (top flight) titles in 1930–31, 1932–33, 1933–34, and 1934–35.
      • Won the FA Cup in 1930 and 1936.
      • Introduced innovations like numbered shirts and floodlights.
    • Chapman tragically passed away in 1934, but his legacy lived on.
  • 1970–71: The Double
    • Under manager Bertie Mee, Arsenal achieved their first "Double" (winning both the league and the FA Cup in the same season).
    • This cemented Arsenal’s status as a top-tier club.
  • 1989: The Anfield Miracle
    • Arsenal won the First Division title in dramatic fashion on the final day of the season.
    • Michael Thomas scored a last-minute goal at Anfield to secure a 2–0 win over Liverpool, clinching the title on goals scored.
  • 2003–04: The Invincibles
    • Managed by Arsène Wenger, Arsenal became the first team in Premier League history to go unbeaten for an entire season.
    • The team finished with 26 wins and 12 draws, earning them the nickname "The Invincibles."
  • 2006: Move to Emirates Stadium
    • After 93 years at Highbury, Arsenal moved to the modern Emirates Stadium in Ashburton Grove.
    • The new stadium has a capacity of over 60,000, making it one of the largest in England.
  • 2017: FA Cup Glory
    • Arsenal won the FA Cup under manager Arsène Wenger, defeating Chelsea 2–1 in the final.
    • This victory marked Wenger’s 7th FA Cup title, the most by any manager in history.
  • 2020s: A New Era
    • Under manager Mikel Arteta, Arsenal returned to the Champions League after several years of absence.
    • The club continues to build a young, dynamic squad with a focus on attacking football and developing homegrown talent.
